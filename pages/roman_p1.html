Roman Theater

The Roman theater in Alexandria in Kom El-Dikka is one of the monuments of the Roman era and was established at the beginning of the fourth century AD . There is another theater in Egypt, which is the Blossom Theater, which is located in the Pelusium antiquities area, 25 km east of the Suez Canal
This building was discovered by chance, while removing dirt to search for the tomb of Alexander the Great by a Polish expedition in 1960 . Archaeologists called it the Roman theater when the marble steps were discovered, but a great controversy arose about the function of this ancient building. It took about 30 years to excavate. The Polish mission continued its mission with the University of Alexandria until some study halls were discovered next to this amphitheater in February 2004 , and this will change the trend that the Roman amphitheater is a theater; This amphitheater may have been used as a large lecture hall for students , and in celebrations it was used as a theater Third

The building is a horseshoe or u-shaped building and consists of 13 rows of marble terraces numbered with Greek letters and numbers to organize the seating process, the first from the bottom, and it can accommodate about 600 people. They are made of pink granite , and there are 5 booths on top of these stands, of which there are only two booths left. The ceiling of these booths had domes based on a group of columns, and the stands were based on a thick wall of limestone surrounded by another wall. The two walls were linked by a group of Arches and vaults where the outer wall is a strong support for the inner wall. There are also two mosaic halls with geometric motifs at the entrance to the west


kom El Shoqafa Cemetery

The catacombs of Kom el Shoqafa is a historical archaeological site located in Alexandria , in the Kom el Shoqafa area south of Mina El Basal neighborhood. It is considered one of the most important cemeteries of the city , and is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Middle Ages. The cemetery consists of a series of Alexandrian tombs, statues, and archaeological remains of the pharaonic funeral cult. Because of the time period at that time, many features of the catacombs in Kom el Shoqafa combine Roman, Greek and Egyptian cultural points. Some of the statues were made in Egyptian styles, but carried Roman styles of clothing and hair style, while others shared a similar style. There is a circular staircase in the middle of it, leading to the tombs that were excavated inside the rock during the era of the Antonine emperors (2nd century AD), and it was mostly used to transport the dead bodies. The importance of the cemetery is due to its vastness, the large number of its decorations, and the complexity of its planning. It is also considered one of the clearest examples of the overlapping of Pharaonic art with Roman art in the city and the finest examples of funerary architecture. The cemetery is of the Katacomb type, a type of cemetery that spread in the first three centuries AD, and the Catacombe cemetery is the main cemetery in the Kom El Shoqafa area. It bore this name due to the similarity in planning between them and the Christian catacombs in Rome, and the name Katacombe, a term used for graves dug under the surface of the earth .

The cemetery consists of a spiral staircase connecting the three floors, and the staircase whose lower steps are higher, then the height of the steps gradually decreases until it is almost non-existent near the surface of the earth . It is considered the largest public Roman tomb that was found in Alexandria. It is located on the borders of the western cemetery in ancient Alexandria, and it dates back to the second century AD . Mast Pole

The Pillar Column is an ancient Roman column located in the city of Alexandria in Egypt , and is one of the most famous monuments in it. It was built on the hill of Bab Sidra, between the present Muslim burial area, known as the Al -Amoud burial ground , and the ancient Kom Al-Shaqafa plateau. It is about 27 meters long and made of red granite . It was held in honor of Emperor Diocletian in the third century AD . It is the last surviving relics of the Temple of the Serapeum , which was set up by Postumus . It is considered the tallest monument in the world. The exact date of the establishment of this column has not been determined, but it dates back to the Roman era, and it was said that this column was dedicated to Christianity after its victory in Alexandria, Ali Miriam in the Battle of the Shirt

he name of the column dates back to the Arab era, where it is believed that it came as a result of the height of this towering column among 400 other columns, which is similar to the masts of ships . The body of the column is a single piece with a diameter of 2.70 meters at the base and 2.30 meters at the crown. The total height of the column, including the base, is about 26.85 meters. On the western side of the column are two bases that can be accessed by an underground ladder. There are also two statues similar to the Sphinx made of pink granite, dating back to the era of Ptolemy VI . On one of them is an inscription of King Horemheb of the Eighteenth Dynasty .
