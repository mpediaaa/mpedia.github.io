Al-Hakim B'amr Allah Mosque

Al-Hakim’s Mosque is a mosque built in 380 AH during the era of Al-Aziz Billah the Fatimid, who began in the year 379 AH (989 AD) the construction of another mosque outside Bab al-Futuh, but he died before its completion, so his son al-Hakim completed it in 403 AH (1012-1013 AD), so it was attributed to him and became known as the Al-Hakim’s Mosque. Its length is 120.5 meters and its width is 113 meters. Its area is less than the area of Amr Ibn Al-Aas Mosque. At the end of its (Northwest) sea facade there are two minarets surrounded by two great pyramid-shaped bases. The walls of the mosque are high, and from each of the two upper cubes there is an octagonal minaret. In the middle of this waterfront and between the two minarets there is the entrance to the ancient mosque, which is the first prominent entrance built in the Cairo Mosque. The arch and the wall in it are newly built. In the entrance on the right and on the left there are remnants of exquisite inscriptions, their height is 1.60 meters. They are a tabernacle at the entrance. The entrance leads to the courtyard of the mosque, which is surrounded by the vessels.

Al-Fustat

Fustat is the first and oldest Islamic capital, as it was built after the Arab Islamic conquest of Egypt in (20 AH / 641 AD). After the Caliph Omar Ibn Al-Khattab refused to take Alexandria as the capital of the country - which was the capital of Egypt throughout the era of the Ptolemies and the Romans - Amr Ibn Al-Aas chose to A site devoid of construction and architecture, except for the Roman fort known as the Qasr al-Shamaa at the Pharaonic city of Memphis for the founding of the new capital. Fustat remained the capital of Egypt for 113 years. Currently, this area is known as the Old Cairo Neighborhood, and it is one of the oldest neighborhoods in Greater Cairo, and includes many archaeological sites, including the Ibn Ezra Synagogue, the churches of ancient Egypt, the Amr Ibn Al-Aas Mosque, the excavations of the ruins of the city of Fustat, the Nilometer on Al-Rawda Island, and the Manasterly Palace. And the palace of Muhammad Ali in Manial.

Amr Ibn Al-Aas Mosque

Amr Ibn Al-Aas Mosque is located in Fustat, in the Old Cairo district. It is the first mosque built in Egypt after Amr ibn al-Aas opened it in the year 20 AH, corresponding to the year 641 AD. This mosque was built in the year 21 AH corresponding to 641 AD, and when it was established, it was a center for governance and a nucleus for the call to the Islamic religion in Egypt. And then the city of Fustat, which is the first capital of Islamic Egypt, was built around it. The site chosen by Amr ibn al-Aas to build this mosque at that time overlooked the Nile, as it was overlooking the Babylon Fortress, which is located next to it. And because this mosque is the first mosque built in Egypt, it was known by several names, including the Old Mosque and the Crown of Mosques.

Prince Muhammad Ali Palace

Prince Muhammad Ali Palace in Manial is one of the palaces of the royal era in Egypt with a special architectural character. The construction of the palace began in 1901. It is located on Manial El-Rawda Island in Cairo, on an area of ​​61,711 square meters, of which 5,000 square meters represents the area of ​​buildings. The palace is a unique architectural masterpiece as it includes various Islamic styles of art between Fatimid, Mamluk, Ottoman, Andalusian, Persian and Shami. It has a wall in the style of the walls of medieval fortresses, while its palaces are surrounded from the inside by gardens that include a rare collection of trees and plants. The palace is currently used as a museum. The palace was owned by Prince Muhammad Ali, the second son of Khedive Tawfiq, and brother of Khedive Abbas Hilmi II. And who held the position of Crown Prince three times, and was one of the three guardians of the throne in the period between the death of King Fuad I and the assumption of his constitutional powers by his cousin King Farouk upon completing the legal age. Prince Muhammad Ali chose the land of the palace himself, and initially established the residence hall, then completed the rest of the palaces.

Ahmed Ibn Tulun Mosque

Ahmed Ibn Tulun Mosque is the third mosque established in Islamic Egypt. It was established by Ahmed Ibn Tulun to be a comprehensive mosque to meet with Muslims in the Friday prayer, and its area is about six and a half acres. In the eastern portico, there is a fragment of a marble plaque that includes the name of the builder and the date of the mosque's establishment written in Kufic script. The mosque consists of a square courtyard in the middle, which is an open courtyard It is surrounded by four galleries, the largest of which is the qibla gallery The mosque is surrounded from the outside by increases on three sides, except for the qibla wall, which was adjacent to the Emirate House, which was established by Ahmed bin Tulun. The mosque is built of red bricks, and in the middle of the mosque’s court is a fountain inside a square-planned building, topped by a dome carried on rows of muqarnas. The mihrab of the mosque is surrounded by two columns, and it is in the form of a semi-circular cavity in the qibla wall. Its walls are made of marble mosaic, topped by a band of glass decorations with inscriptions in the Naskhi script. In addition to the large mihrab, there are five other stucco warriors painted on the walls. The height of the mosque’s minaret is 40.44 meters from the surface of the earth, and the external facade system of the mosque is taken from the design of the facade of the Amr ibn al-Aas mosque during the reign of Abdullah ibn Taher, the Abbasid governor, in the year 212 AH.
